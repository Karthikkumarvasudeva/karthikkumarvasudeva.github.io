01 — About

Bridging rigorous mathematics
with scalable computation

I am a Doctoral Candidate and Instructor of Record in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics at Texas Tech University. Under the guidance of Dr. Eugenio Aulisa (Chair) and Dr. Giorgio Bornia (Co-Chair), my research focuses on the development and analysis of Finite Element Methods for Boundary Control Problems Constrained by the Mixed Biharmonic Equation. My work aims to bridge the gap between high-order partial differential equations and robust numerical implementations for complex control systems.

My research sits at the intersection of numerical analysis, PDE-constrained optimization, and scientific computing. I design mixed finite element formulations, prove well-posedness and error estimates in Sobolev spaces, and implement large-scale sparse saddle-point solvers in C++ combining mathematical depth with engineering-grade software.

As Instructor of Record for classes of 90–120 undergraduate students, I have designed full courses — from College Algebra through Calculus III — including syllabi, lectures, exams, and computational assignments. I believe rigorous mathematics should be taught with clarity, enthusiasm, and purpose.

03 — Research & Publications

Finite elements for optimal control

Dissertation: Finite Element Methods for Boundary Control Problems Constrained by the Mixed Biharmonic Equation

Developing mixed finite element discretizations for PDE-constrained optimal control problems governed by fourth-order elliptic equations. Includes deriving well-posedness in Sobolev spaces, proving a priori error estimates, implementing lifting-function techniques for Dirichlet boundary controls, and building scalable C++ solvers for large sparse saddle-point systems using Hermann–Miyoshi and Ciarlet–Raviart discretizations.

05 — Projects

Computational work that matters

01
C++ · FEM · Saddle-Point
Boundary Optimal Control for Biharmonic Equations
Mixed FEM solvers for boundary optimal control constrained by the biharmonic equation. Hermann–Miyoshi and Ciarlet–Raviart discretizations, lifting-function techniques, optimal convergence on large sparse systems.
02
C++ · Variational Formulations
Distributed Optimal Control via FEM
Distributed optimal control solvers for elliptic and biharmonic PDEs — error estimation, convergence studies, large-scale sparse linear systems with performance-aware scientific programming.
03
C++ · Inf-Sup Stability
Mixed FEM for Higher-Order PDEs
Mixed formulations for fourth-order PDEs reducing regularity requirements. Block-matrix structures, inf-sup stability, and scalable solver design connecting variational formulations with numerical linear algebra.
04
Python · PyTorch · PINN
Neural Network Solvers for DEs
Physics-informed neural networks approximating ODE solutions. Loss-based training, GPU-accelerated optimization, automatic differentiation — bridging classical numerical analysis with modern ML.
05
Python · Parallel · HPC
Parallel QR Factorization
QR Factorization with partial and full pivoting using shared-memory parallelism. Numerical stability analysis, flop counting, and scalability study relevant to GPU-accelerated HPC architectures.
06
C++ · JavaScript · p5.js
Computational STEM Programming
Designed hands-on C++ and JavaScript modules at TexPREP — Morse-code translators to Mandelbrot set visualizations — connecting mathematical modeling, algorithms, and scalable computation.
